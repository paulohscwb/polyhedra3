  <h2 align="center"><img src="vr/salas/imagens/icone.png" style="margin-bottom:-10px" width="45"> Dragon fractals of non-convex polyhedra</h2>
The dragon curve belongs to the family of self-similar fractal curves, which can be approximated by recursive methods. The dragon curve is probably most commonly thought of as the shape that is generated from a strip of paper repeatedly folded in half.
<br>This work shows non-convex polyhedra forming dragon fractals, modeled for visualization in Virtual Reality.

<h4>1. Iris octagonal antitoroid</h4>
<a href="vr/dragon1.htm" target="_blank" title="3D model" class="fotoA"><img src="ar/1A.png" class="foto" alt="Iris octagonal antitoroid dragon fractal"></a>
 <br>Applying the construction principle of the dragon curve with the Iris octagonal antitoroid, we obtain an iris octagonal antitoroid dragon fractal. In the first order of construction of the fractal, we construct two new iris octagonal antitoroids corresponding to one original polyhedron. In this example, we have solid representations in orders from 0 to 10.
